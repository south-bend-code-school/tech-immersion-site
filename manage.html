Management

At the start of class . . .

  1. If you have a new student, have all the students sit together.
  2. One instructor should be with the students, while the other is in charge of check-in.
  3. Start Students with a prompt.
    1. For Younger Students, SB likes to start with a drawing prompt.
    2. For Older Students, start with a question, to get a group discussion started.

During class . . .

  1. Once the majority of students have arrived, review everyone's drawing/question prompt.
  2. Explain today's schedule and begin to get laptops for each student.
  3. Mitigate questions from students.
    1. Be available for students' questions.
    2. Note: Not every student will be confident enough to ask questions, so float and check-in on each student, periodically.

At the end of class . . .

  1. Show a clean up PowerPoint Slide (which is attached to the bottom of the drawing prompt slideshow). This is an easy way to notify all students that class is ending and will help the clean up process much faster.
